Caramelized Pork Bowl

Vietnamese Caramelized Ground Pork Bowl

Are you tired of going to the grocery store only to leave with the same boring meals you've had repeatedly over the last few weeks? I've been there! I started browsing the meat department and saw ground pork... what could I do with that? So I looked it up and found this delicious sweet and savory caramelized pork recipe.

I have done multiple takes on this recipe personally and suggest you do the same! The standard recipe source recommends you use fish sauce but I substitute soy sauce. I also keep adding the "sauce" base ingredients until I get an amount I like. To make a version of the pork bowl like an egg roll bowl, throw in your rice and a package of coleslaw mix!

Ingredients

  • cooking oil
  • 1/2 onion
  • 2 tsp ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • thai chili (finely chopped)(I use chili powder)
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 5 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or soy sauce)

For Serving/Optional

  • rice
  • cole slaw mix
  • green onion (sliced to garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat your cooking oil over med to high heat.
  2. Add chopped onion, ginger, garlic, and chili. Cook for approximately 2 minutes.
  3. Add ground pork, break up the meat and cook until white all over.
  4. Add brown sugar, fish/soy sauce. Stir, and then let cook without touching until the juices start to cook out and the pork begins to caramelize.
  5. Stir and leave it again to get more caramelization and repeat this step until satisfied. Feel free to repeat this step and the step prior until satisfied with the amount of caramelization and sauce.
  6. Serve over rice, or stir in your rice and cole slaw mix for a caramelized pork egg roll bowl.
  7. Garnish and enjoy!
